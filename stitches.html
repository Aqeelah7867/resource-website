Types of stitches in embroiderery

Embroidery stitches are the foundation of every design, creating texture, detail, and dimension in your work.Mastering these stitches will allow you to create intricate and stunning embroidery designs. 1. Outline Stitches (Used for Borders & Lines)

Backstitch:

  • Creates a solid, continuous line.
  • Used for outlines, lettering, and fine details.

    Running Stitch

  • A simple dashed-line stitch.
  • Great for decorative details and quilting.

    • Hover over each step by step image for a more in depth youtube tutorial!

    Step 1 - Knot the Thread Step 2 - First Stitch Step 3 - Stitch Backwards Step 4 - Pull Needle Through Step 5 - leave 0.5cm space and repeat

    2. Filling Stitches (Used for Large Areas & Texture)

    Satin Stitch

  • Creates a smooth, shiny, solid fill.
  • Used for letters, petals, and shapes.

    • Hover over each step by step image for a more in depth youtube tutorial!

    Step 1 - Knot the Thread Step 2 - First Stitch in a straight line Step 3 - it will always be starting from the opposite direction you started from Step 4 - make sure you are stitching very close to the previous stitch 5 - fill desired shape

    3. Decorative Stitches(Used for Embellishments & Accents)

    French Knot

  • Creates a small, raised knot.
  • Used for flower centers, eyes, and textures.

    • Hover over each step by step image for a more in depth youtube tutorial!

    Step 1 - Knot the Thread Step 2 - pull needle from under fabric up all the way Step 3 - take thread at top and wrap around the edge of the needle three times tightly Step 4 - turn needle to face down going through the same place you just brought it up from 5 - pull through tightly

    Lazy Daisy (Detached Chain Stitch)

  • Forms petal-like loops.
  • Perfect for flowers and leaf patterns.

    • Hover over each step by step image for a more in depth youtube tutorial!

    Step 1 - Knot the Thread Step 2 - create a loose loop Step 3 - hold open with finger and insert thread back up at point 1 to two across the top of the loop Step 4 - pull down tightly to secure the knot 5 - repeat for the other petals next to the existing loop

    Bullion Knot

  • A longer, wrapped knot.
  • Used for roses, curls, and textured details.

    • Tutorial from Practical Embroiderery on youtube check them out!

    Hover over each step by step image for a more in depth youtube tutorial!

    Step 1 - Knot the Thread Step 2 - First, put needle halfway through fabric, enough that the top of the needle is visble but don't pull completely through Step 3 - take the thread at the top and wrap around 8 times, and hold it with the pad of your finger pressing down Step 4 - slowly pull through, upwards so that the knot tightens 5 - pull through and secure knot
