Types of stitches in embroiderery

Embroidery stitches are the foundation of every design, creating texture, detail, and dimension in your work.Mastering these stitches will allow you to create intricate and stunning embroidery designs. 1. Outline Stitches (Used for Borders & Lines)

Backstitch:

  • Creates a solid, continuous line.
  • Used for outlines, lettering, and fine details.

    • Running Stitch

  • A simple dashed-line stitch.
  • Great for decorative details and quilting.

    • 2. Filling Stitches (Used for Large Areas & Texture)

    Satin Stitch

  • Creates a smooth, shiny, solid fill.
  • Used for letters, petals, and shapes.

    • 3. Decorative Stitches(Used for Embellishments & Accents)

    French Knot

  • Creates a small, raised knot.
  • Used for flower centers, eyes, and textures.

    • Lazy Daisy (Detached Chain Stitch)

  • Forms petal-like loops.
  • Perfect for flowers and leaf patterns.

    • Bullion Knot

  • A longer, wrapped knot.
  • Used for roses, curls, and textured details.
